Summary

This course is an introduction to mathematical logic.  We characterize the notion of computability and will rigorously prove completeness theorems and other results for first-order predicate logic.  See syllabus for detailed description.

Evaluation:  Four problem sets (20%), five quizzes (counting best four) (15%), mid-term test (25%), final exam (40%).

Text: G.S. Boolos, J.P. Burgess and R.C. Jeffrey, Computability and Logic, 5th edition (Cambridge, 2007).  [Note:  Used copies of 4th edition are almost identical and are fine for the course.  Even the pagination is almost the same as 5th edition.  But there are a few changes to the problems.  I have indicated a few changes here, though please let me know if you spot others.]
